about us

We focus on sheltering strays that has been neglected in the streets

Safe Space for Strays is not just merely an organization but a safe home for animals that was unfortunate in the beginning of their lives. We believe that in this big world, strays should also exist together with good people because their lives also matter.

In this website, the chosen content about a sheltering home is the Strays Worth Saving (SWS) organization.

"SWS is dedicated to giving animals who are in extreme pain a second chance at life. We rescue strays who are suffering in the streets with no one to take care of them. We also rescue pets who are abused, neglected, or abandoned by their owners. Occasionally, we financially assist owners who love their pets but cannot afford treatment for painful diseases." --Strays Worh Saving

Offered Services

Rescue

We give a second chance to animals who are in extreme pain. These are strays who are all alone with no one to care for them. We bring them to a trusted vet and fund their treatment.

Shelter

Our shelter in Tanauan, Batangas takes care of over 100 rescues who are waiting for a forever home. They are fed thrice a day, cleaned, and have space to play.

Adoption

We work hard to find forever homes for our rescues. SWS adopters are strictly screened to ensure that they can give our rescues a good life.

Spay & Neuter

SWS's ultimate dream is a stray-free Philippines. We promote Spay and Neuter and offer low-cost options.

Vaccine Awarenesss

Every cat and dog deserves to live a long and healthy life. We advocate for responsible pet ownership through vaccinations.

Our Updates

On Estee

Estee is going to be transferred to an ortho vet for her surgery. She is also blind in 1 eye. We hope the surgery goes well. Please continue supporting and praying for her recovery and vetting.

This dog is reported to SWS by Karen Faith to have been in the same area for a week with no rescue. It is claimed to have been hit by somebody on the head and leg. This is the reason the dog does not leave her location. She is so afraid she just hides under it.

Estee has been confined since yesterday. An x-ray showed that one leg is dislocated and surgery might be necessary to repair the problem. She is also blind on the left eye.

Tests, medications, and treatment are now being administered for this poor dog. I would like to request your support for all our rescues, especially those confined, as this is where the bulk of expense is.

Running Bill: P8,000

On Akira

Akira is getting better but still needs medication. Please pray for his continuous recovery. To those who can share with Akira's vetting, we will deeply appreciate it.

This dog is being taken cared of by an 85 year old who loves Akira but has no funds for vetting.

Akira developed hernia. This makes it difficult for her to have her bowel movement. This can grown and become much bigger if left alone. So to arrest the problem, I agreed to fundraise the vetting.

Medications will first be administered to try to heal the inguinal hernia. If after a week of observation there remains to be no improvement, then an operation will be necessary.

When did you start rescuing?

It was in 2020 when SWS started rescuing strays and animals. Now, it has been only two years since SWS started. The journey has been fast but it was fruitful and very meaningful. The initial plan was just to save dogs or cats one at a time.

Today, SWS has more than 660,000 followers and has rescued more than 1,500 dogs and cats. We have also turned into a foundation and built a shelter, the SWS Tanauan, Batangas shelter.
